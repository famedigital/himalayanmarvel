Tiger's Nest Monastery
Alt: 3,120m
Hike: 4-5 hours
Climb: 900m

Today we visit Bhutan's most iconic landmark: Taktsang Palphug Monastery (Tiger's Nest), perched dramatically on a cliff 900 meters above the valley floor. Legend says Guru Rinpoche flew here on the back of a tigress. The hike takes approximately 4-5 hours round trip through beautiful pine forests. Visit the main temple, photograph the breathtaking views, and enjoy lunch at the cafeteria with panoramic views of the monastery.

"A sacred journey to one of Buddhism's most holy sites, clinging to a cliff face in the Himalayas."

Responsible Travel
Do's & Don'ts
✓ Do's
  • Respect local customs and traditions at all times
  • Dress modestly – especially when visiting temples and dzongs
  • Ask permission before taking photographs of people
  • Carry reusable water bottles – reduce plastic waste
  • Support local businesses by buying local handicrafts
  • Learn basic Bhutanese greetings – "Kuzu zangpo la"
  • Stay on marked trails to protect alpine vegetation
  • Remove your hat and sunglasses before entering temples
  • Walk clockwise around chortens and mani walls
  • Carry small Rupee/Ngultrum notes for offerings
✗ Don'ts
  • Don't disrespect religious objects or customs
  • Don't wear hats inside temples and monasteries
  • Don't point at people with your finger
  • Don't litter – carry out all waste
  • Don't buy antiques – illegal to export from Bhutan
  • Don't smoke in public places – strict tobacco laws
  • Don't give money, candy, or pens to children
  • Don't touch people's heads – considered sacred
  • Don't enter temples without permission
  • Don't take photos inside temples without permission
