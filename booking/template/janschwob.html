Photography Opportunities
Paro Valley aerial views Tashichho Dzong at twilight Thimphu street scenes Traditional architecture
02
Thimphu → Punakha • Mountain Pass Journey
Drive: ~3 hours
High Point: Dochula La 3,150m
Night: Punakha
Morning drive over Dochula Pass—a mountain saddle adorned with 108 chortens (stupas). On clear days, capture panoramic views of the entire eastern Himalayan range. This is one of Bhutan's best landscape photography locations. Descend into the warmer Punakha Valley and visit the magnificent Punakha Dzong, arguably Bhutan's most beautiful fortress, situated at the confluence of two rivers.
Photography Opportunities
Dochula 108 Stupas + Himalayas Punakha Dzong architecture River confluence views Mountain road landscapes
03
Punakha → Phobjikha • Crane Valley
Drive: ~3 hours
Altitude: 3,000m
Night: Phobjikha
Drive into the high-altitude glacial valley of Phobjikha—a protected wetland and winter home to the endangered Black-necked Cranes. These sacred birds arrive from Tibet in late October and stay until March. Evening photography session focused on the cranes as they return to roost. Visit the Gangtey Monastery overlooking the valley.
📷 Black-necked Crane Photography Tips
Cranes are best photographed in early morning (sunrise) or late afternoon (sunset). Use a telephoto lens (200mm+). The cranes perform their famous mating dances—be patient and ready!
Photography Opportunities
Black-necked Cranes at sunset Gangtey Dzong panorama Glacial valley landscapes Traditional village life

05
Paro • Tiger's Nest Trek ★
Hike: 4-5 hours
Altitude: 3,120m
Night: Paro
The big day! Trek to Bhutan's most iconic landmark—Taktsang Palphug Monastery (Tiger's Nest), perched dramatically on a cliff 900 meters above the valley floor. Legend says Guru Rinpoche flew here on the back of a tigress. For photography, we recommend starting early (7:30 AM) to capture the best morning light on the monastery walls before harsh midday sun. Visit the main temple, photograph breathtaking views, and enjoy lunch at the cafeteria. Afternoon free for souvenir shopping.
📷 Tiger's Nest Photography Tips
Start early to avoid crowds and capture soft morning light. Best viewpoints are at the cafeteria (mid-way) and from the main temple. Use wide-angle for the monastery, telephoto for details. Prayer flags add beautiful foreground elements.
Photography Opportunities
Tiger's Nest viewpoints Cliff-side monastery details Prayer flags & panoramas Valley floor views

Important Information
Terms & Conditions
Booking & Payment

A 30% non-refundable deposit is required to confirm your booking. The remaining balance must be paid 30 days before your arrival date. We accept wire transfers and all major credit cards.

Cancellation Policy

Full refund (minus deposit) for cancellations made 60+ days before departure. 50% refund for cancellations 30-60 days before. No refund within 30 days of departure unless covered by travel insurance.

Visa & SDF

The SDF ($100/night) and Visa fee ($40) are government-mandated. These are paid directly during the booking process. We ensure all paperwork is submitted once your deposit is confirmed. SDF is included in the package rate.

Travel Documents

Valid passports (minimum 6 months validity) are required for all travelers. We will process your Bhutan visa upon booking confirmation. Ensure you have the necessary permits for photography in restricted areas.

Health & Fitness

The Tiger's Nest trek is moderate to strenuous. A reasonable level of fitness is required. We recommend consulting your doctor before undertaking high-altitude trekking. Inform us of any medical conditions in advance.

Liability

Silverpine Bhutan acts as an agent for hotels, transport, and service providers. We shall not be liable for any injury, damage, loss, delay, or irregularity caused by defects in services or acts of God.

Photography Guidelines

Photography is prohibited inside most temples and dzongs. Always ask permission before photographing people, especially monks and religious practitioners. Drone photography requires special permits—please inform us in advance if you plan to bring a drone.

Weather Considerations

November offers the clearest skies and best visibility. Daytime temperatures range from 15-20°C in valleys, nights can be cooler. Pack layers. We maintain flexibility for golden hour photography sessions.

Photography-Specific Notes
• Our guides are photography-aware and will accommodate golden hour requests
• Vehicle storage for camera equipment available
• Tripods welcome (please carry your own)
• Power outlets in hotels for charging equipment
• We recommend carrying backup batteries and memory cards
